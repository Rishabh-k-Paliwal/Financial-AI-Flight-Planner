INSURANCE FUNDAMENTALS
=======================

WHY INSURANCE IS A FINANCIAL PRIORITY
--------------------------------------
Insurance is the shield that protects your financial plan. Without proper coverage,
a single medical emergency, accident, or death can wipe out years of savings and
leave your family in financial ruin. Insurance should be addressed BEFORE aggressive
investing.

HEALTH INSURANCE (Priority #1)
-------------------------------
This is non-negotiable. Medical debt is the #1 cause of bankruptcy in the US.

Options:
• Employer-sponsored plan (usually cheapest due to employer subsidy)
• ACA Marketplace (Healthcare.gov) — subsidies available based on income
• COBRA (temporary — up to 18 months after leaving a job, but expensive)
• Medicaid (if income qualifies)

Key terms:
• Premium: Monthly cost to maintain coverage
• Deductible: Amount you pay before insurance kicks in
• Copay/Coinsurance: Your share of costs after deductible
• Out-of-Pocket Maximum: The most you'll pay in a year

HSA (Health Savings Account):
If you have a high-deductible health plan (HDHP), an HSA is a triple-tax-advantaged
account: tax-deductible contributions, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawals for
medical expenses. Max contribution: $4,150 individual / $8,300 family (2024).

LIFE INSURANCE (Priority #2)
------------------------------
Needed if anyone depends on your income (spouse, children, aging parents).

Types:
• TERM LIFE: Pure insurance for a set period (10, 20, 30 years). Affordable.
  Recommended for most people. Buy 10-12× your annual income in coverage.
  
• WHOLE LIFE / UNIVERSAL LIFE: Combined insurance + investment.
  Generally NOT recommended — high fees, low returns.
  Term + invest the difference is almost always better.

Rule of thumb: Coverage = 10-12× annual income
Example: $60,000 income → $600,000-$720,000 term life policy
A healthy 30-year-old can get a 20-year, $500,000 term policy for ~$25-35/month.

DISABILITY INSURANCE (Priority #3)
------------------------------------
Often overlooked but critically important. You are MORE likely to become
disabled than to die during your working years.

Types:
• Short-term disability: Covers 3-6 months (often employer-provided)
• Long-term disability: Covers years until retirement if needed

Target: Replace 60-70% of your gross income.
Check if your employer provides it — many do at no cost. If not, buy a policy.

WHEN TO SKIP OR REDUCE INSURANCE
----------------------------------
• Life insurance: If you're single with no dependents, you may not need it.
• Disability: If your savings can cover 6+ months, short-term may be optional.
• Never skip health insurance. Ever.

ACTION ITEMS FOR THE UNINSURED
-------------------------------
1. If no health insurance → Go to Healthcare.gov during open enrollment
   or apply for Medicaid. This is URGENT.
2. If no life insurance but have dependents → Get 3 term life quotes today.
   Try Policygenius.com or your employer's benefits portal.
3. If no disability insurance → Check your employer's benefits first.
   If not available, contact a disability insurance broker.
