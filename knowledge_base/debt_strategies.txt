DEBT ELIMINATION STRATEGIES
===========================

UNDERSTANDING DEBT TYPES
------------------------
• TOXIC DEBT (>15% APR): Credit cards, payday loans, store cards. 
  This debt is a financial emergency. Minimum $200-500/month extra payments required.
  
• HIGH-INTEREST DEBT (7-15%): Personal loans, some car loans, private student loans.
  Aggressive paydown recommended before investing beyond employer match.
  
• MODERATE DEBT (4-7%): Federal student loans, some car loans.
  Balance between paydown and investing. Consider refinancing.
  
• LOW-INTEREST DEBT (<4%): Mortgages, some federal student loans.
  Minimum payments are fine — invest the difference instead.

THE AVALANCHE METHOD (Mathematically Optimal)
---------------------------------------------
1. List all debts from HIGHEST to LOWEST interest rate.
2. Pay minimum on every debt.
3. Throw ALL extra money at the debt with the highest rate.
4. When that debt is paid off, roll that payment into the next highest rate.
5. Repeat until debt-free.

ADVANTAGE: Saves the most money on interest. Fastest path to debt freedom.
BEST FOR: People motivated by math and long-term optimization.

Example:
- Credit Card A: $5,000 @ 22% → ATTACK THIS FIRST
- Credit Card B: $3,000 @ 18% → Attack second
- Car Loan: $15,000 @ 5% → Minimum payments
- Student Loan: $30,000 @ 4% → Minimum payments

THE SNOWBALL METHOD (Psychologically Powerful)
----------------------------------------------
1. List all debts from SMALLEST to LARGEST balance.
2. Pay minimum on every debt.
3. Throw ALL extra money at the smallest balance.
4. When paid off, roll that payment to the next smallest.
5. Repeat until debt-free.

ADVANTAGE: Quick wins create momentum and motivation.
BEST FOR: People who need psychological victories to stay on track.

DEBT-TO-INCOME RATIO (DTI)
---------------------------
Your monthly debt payments ÷ gross monthly income = DTI

• Under 20%: Healthy — you're in good shape.
• 20-35%: Manageable — room for improvement.
• 35-50%: Stressed — need a paydown plan immediately.
• Over 50%: Crisis — consider debt consolidation or counseling.

REFINANCING CONSIDERATIONS
---------------------------
• Only refinance if the new rate saves at least 1-2% AND you plan to keep the loan.
• Federal student loans: Be cautious — refinancing loses income-driven repayment options.
• Mortgage refinancing: Factor in closing costs (typically 2-5% of loan amount).

MONTHLY PAYMENT FORMULA FOR DEBT FREEDOM
-----------------------------------------
To become debt-free in N months:
Payment = Balance × (Rate/12) / (1 - (1 + Rate/12)^(-N))

Example: $10,000 at 18% APR, paid off in 24 months:
Payment = $10,000 × 0.015 / (1 - 1.015^(-24)) = $499/month
