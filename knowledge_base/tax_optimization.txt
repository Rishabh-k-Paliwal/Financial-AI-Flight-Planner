TAX OPTIMIZATION STRATEGIES
============================

TAX-ADVANTAGED ACCOUNTS (Use These First!)
------------------------------------------
These accounts should be maximized BEFORE investing in taxable brokerage accounts.

1. 401(k) / 403(b) — Employer Retirement
   • 2024 Limit: $23,500 (under 50) / $31,000 (50+)
   • Traditional: Tax deduction today, taxed at withdrawal
   • Roth 401(k): No deduction today, tax-free at withdrawal
   • ALWAYS get the full employer match — this is free money

2. IRA (Individual Retirement Account)
   • 2024 Limit: $7,000 (under 50) / $8,000 (50+)
   • Traditional IRA: Tax-deductible if income qualifies
   • Roth IRA: No deduction, but tax-free growth and withdrawal
   • Roth IRA income limits: $161,000 single / $240,000 married (2024)

3. HSA (Health Savings Account) — "Stealth IRA"
   • 2024 Limit: $4,150 individual / $8,300 family
   • Triple tax advantage: deductible, tax-free growth, tax-free medical withdrawals
   • After age 65, withdrawals for any purpose are taxed like a Traditional IRA
   • Best strategy: Pay medical expenses out-of-pocket, let HSA grow

TAX BRACKET AWARENESS
----------------------
Understanding your marginal vs. effective tax rate is crucial:

• Marginal rate: The rate on your NEXT dollar of income
• Effective rate: Total tax ÷ total income (always lower)

2024 Federal Tax Brackets (Single):
• 10%: $0 - $11,600
• 12%: $11,601 - $47,150
• 22%: $47,151 - $100,525
• 24%: $100,526 - $191,950
• 32%: $191,951 - $243,725
• 35%: $243,726 - $609,350
• 37%: Over $609,350

ROTH vs. TRADITIONAL DECISION
-------------------------------
• Choose ROTH if: You expect to be in a HIGHER tax bracket in retirement
  (younger workers, lower income now, expect career growth)
• Choose TRADITIONAL if: You expect to be in a LOWER tax bracket in retirement
  (high earners near peak, planning to retire in low-cost area)
• When in doubt: Split contributions 50/50 for tax diversification

TAX-LOSS HARVESTING (Taxable Accounts)
---------------------------------------
In taxable brokerage accounts, you can sell investments at a loss to offset gains:
• Offset capital gains dollar-for-dollar
• Offset up to $3,000 of ordinary income per year
• Carry forward unused losses indefinitely
• Beware the "wash sale rule" — can't buy substantially identical securities
  within 30 days before or after the sale

EMPLOYER MATCH PRIORITY
------------------------
Before ANY other tax optimization, ensure you're capturing 100% of your
employer's 401(k) match. Typical matches:
• 50% match up to 6% of salary = 3% free money
• 100% match up to 3% of salary = 3% free money
• Dollar-for-dollar up to 4% = 4% free money

If your employer matches 50% of 6%, and you earn $60,000:
• You contribute 6% = $3,600/year
• Employer adds 3% = $1,800/year (FREE!)
• Total going to retirement = $5,400/year
