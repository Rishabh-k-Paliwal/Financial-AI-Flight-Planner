INVESTMENT PRINCIPLES & ASSET ALLOCATION
=========================================

THE 100-AGE RULE (Equity Allocation)
-------------------------------------
A classic rule of thumb for determining how much of your portfolio should be
in stocks (equities) versus bonds:

    Equity Allocation % = 100 - Your Age

Examples:
• Age 25 → 75% stocks, 25% bonds
• Age 35 → 65% stocks, 35% bonds
• Age 45 → 55% stocks, 45% bonds
• Age 55 → 45% stocks, 55% bonds
• Age 65 → 35% stocks, 65% bonds

Modern variation (longer lifespans): Some advisors now use 110 or 120 minus age,
since people live longer and need more growth. For aggressive investors:
    Equity % = 110 - Age  or  120 - Age

FIDELITY RETIREMENT MILESTONES
-------------------------------
How much you should have saved as a multiple of your annual salary:

Age 25: 0.5× salary saved
Age 30: 1× salary
Age 35: 2× salary
Age 40: 3× salary
Age 45: 4× salary
Age 50: 6× salary
Age 55: 7× salary
Age 60: 8× salary
Age 67: 10× salary

If you're behind, increase your savings rate by at least 1% per year until you
catch up. Consider delaying retirement or finding supplemental income.

THE SAFE WITHDRAWAL RATE (4% Rule)
------------------------------------
The Trinity Study (1998) established the "4% Rule":
• In your first year of retirement, withdraw 4% of your total portfolio.
• Each subsequent year, adjust for inflation.
• With a balanced portfolio, this approach has a >95% success rate over 30 years.

Example: 
• Portfolio: $1,000,000
• First-year withdrawal: $40,000/year ($3,333/month)
• Year 2 with 3% inflation: $41,200/year

Modern adjustments:
• Some researchers suggest 3.5% for longer retirements (40+ years).
• Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) may force higher withdrawals later.

LOW-COST INDEX FUND STRATEGY (Three-Fund Portfolio)
----------------------------------------------------
The simplest, most effective investment approach:

1. US Total Stock Market Index Fund (e.g., VTI, VTSAX)
   - Covers 4,000+ US stocks
   - Expense ratio: ~0.03%

2. International Stock Index Fund (e.g., VXUS, VTIAX)
   - Covers developed + emerging markets
   - Expense ratio: ~0.07%

3. US Total Bond Market Index Fund (e.g., BND, VBTLX)
   - Provides stability and income
   - Expense ratio: ~0.03%

Allocation guidance:
• Young (under 40): 60% US Stock, 25% International, 15% Bond
• Middle (40-55): 50% US Stock, 20% International, 30% Bond
• Near Retirement (55+): 35% US Stock, 15% International, 50% Bond

DOLLAR-COST AVERAGING
----------------------
Invest a fixed amount on a regular schedule regardless of market conditions.
This removes the temptation to time the market and smooths out volatility.

Example: Invest $500 on the 1st of every month into your index funds.
When prices are high, you buy fewer shares. When prices are low, you buy more.
Over time, your average cost per share tends to be lower.
