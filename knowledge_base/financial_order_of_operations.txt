THE FINANCIAL ORDER OF OPERATIONS (FOO)
========================================

The Financial Order of Operations is a proven, step-by-step framework for building
wealth. Just like math has PEMDAS, personal finance has its own order that ensures
you build a rock-solid financial foundation before reaching for higher returns.

STEP 1: BUILD A STARTER EMERGENCY FUND ($1,000 - $2,500)
---------------------------------------------------------
Before doing anything else, set aside a small emergency cushion. This prevents you
from going into debt when a surprise car repair or medical bill hits. Even $1,000
can be the difference between a minor inconvenience and a financial spiral.

STEP 2: CAPTURE YOUR EMPLOYER 401(k) MATCH
-------------------------------------------
If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to get the full
match. This is literally a 100% return on your money — no investment on Earth
beats free money. A typical match is 50% of contributions up to 6% of salary.

STEP 3: ELIMINATE HIGH-INTEREST DEBT (> 6-7%)
---------------------------------------------
Credit cards, personal loans, and any debt charging more than 6-7% interest must
be aggressively paid down. The guaranteed "return" from eliminating 20% APR credit
card debt is better than any stock market gamble.

Two proven strategies:
• AVALANCHE METHOD: Pay minimums on everything, throw extra money at the
  highest-rate debt first. Mathematically optimal — saves the most interest.
• SNOWBALL METHOD: Pay minimums on everything, knock out the smallest
  balance first. Psychologically powerful — quick wins build momentum.

STEP 4: BUILD A FULL EMERGENCY FUND (3-6 MONTHS)
-------------------------------------------------
Now save 3-6 months of essential living expenses in a high-yield savings account.
This is your safety net against job loss, disability, or major expenses. Keep it
liquid — not invested in stocks.

Calculation: Monthly Essential Expenses × 6 = Target Emergency Fund

STEP 5: MAXIMIZE TAX-ADVANTAGED RETIREMENT (Roth IRA, HSA)
----------------------------------------------------------
Max out your Roth IRA ($7,000/year in 2024, $8,000 if over 50) and Health Savings
Account if eligible. These accounts offer tax-free growth — your future self will
thank you.

STEP 6: MAX OUT EMPLOYER RETIREMENT (401(k) to $23,500)
--------------------------------------------------------
Push your 401(k) contributions to the annual maximum. This reduces your taxable
income today and compounds tax-deferred for decades.

STEP 7: BUILD WEALTH IN TAXABLE ACCOUNTS
-----------------------------------------
Once all tax-advantaged buckets are full, invest in a taxable brokerage account.
Use low-cost index funds (Total Stock Market, International, Bonds). Focus on
tax-efficient placement.

STEP 8: PREPAY LOW-INTEREST DEBT (Mortgage)
--------------------------------------------
Only after steps 1-7 should you consider paying extra on your mortgage or other
low-rate debt. The math often favors investing over prepaying a 3-5% mortgage.

KEY PRINCIPLES
--------------
• SAVE at least 20-25% of gross income for retirement.
• NEVER withdraw from retirement accounts early (10% penalty + taxes).
• AUTOMATE everything — savings, investments, bill payments.
• REVIEW your financial plan at least quarterly.
• PROTECT yourself with adequate insurance before investing aggressively.
